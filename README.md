# Data Economics: Geopolitical Stress & Market Resilience (2025-2026)

## 🎯 Project Overview
This project investigates the impact of the **2026 Israel-Iran conflict** on the Brazilian market, challenging the common narrative of a "new structural break." 

By combining **Classical Econometrics** with **Machine Learning**, I demonstrate that the Brazilian market's "Physics" actually shifted in **April 2025**. The 2026 events are not a new regime, but a confirmation of the high-stress, high-commodity environment established a year prior.

---

## 🧠 The "Data Economist" Methodology
I believe in bridging the gap between raw code and economic intuition. This analysis follows a strict non-linear workflow:

1. **Dimensionality Reduction (PCA):** Identifying the DNA of the market through two main components:
   - **PC1 (Sentiment):** Driven by USD and DXY.
   - **PC2 (Risk/Volatility):** Dominated by Gold and Brent Oil.
2. **Regime Identification (K-Means Clustering):** Mapping how the market "moves" between different neighborhoods of risk.
3. **Structural Break Testing:** Utilizing **Zivot-Andrews** and **Markov Switching Models** to validate if the 2026 conflict caused a statistical shift (Spoiler: It didn't).

---

## 📈 Key Insights
- **Regime Continuity:** The 2026 data points (red X in the scatter plot) are nested within the 2025 cluster. 
- **Antifragility:** The Brazilian market, as a major commodity exporter with dollarized assets (e.g., Petrobras), showed extreme resilience to the 2026 shock because it was already priced for chaos.
- **No Data Slashing:** This analysis uses **Levels** instead of differentiated series (.diff()) to preserve the "memory" of the economic regime.

> *"I prefer a real and bitter result to a sweet statistical lie. I ain't a data slasher."*
